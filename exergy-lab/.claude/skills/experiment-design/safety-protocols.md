# Safety Protocols by Domain

## General Laboratory Safety

### Minimum Requirements (All Experiments)
- Safety glasses with side shields
- Lab coat (flame-resistant for fire hazards)
- Closed-toe shoes
- Hair tied back
- No food/drink in lab

### Emergency Procedures
1. **Chemical spill**: Evacuate, notify, contain if safe
2. **Fire**: RACE (Rescue, Alarm, Contain, Evacuate)
3. **Injury**: First aid, report, seek medical attention
4. **Equipment failure**: Power off, isolate, report

## Solar Cell Fabrication

### Perovskite Processing
**Hazards**:
- Lead compounds (PbI₂, PbBr₂): Toxic, accumulative
- DMF, DMSO: Skin absorption, liver toxicity
- Chlorobenzene: Carcinogenic

**Required PPE**:
- Nitrile gloves (double glove for Pb)
- Full face shield
- Lab coat with closed cuffs

**Engineering Controls**:
- Fume hood for all solution processing
- Dedicated waste stream for Pb-containing materials
- HEPA filtration for powder handling

**Waste Disposal**:
- Lead waste: Designated hazardous waste container
- Organic solvents: Halogenated/non-halogenated separation

### Thin Film Deposition
**Hazards**:
- Sputtering: High voltage, target materials
- Evaporation: High temperature, vacuum
- PECVD: Toxic gases (silane, ammonia)

**Required Controls**:
- Interlocked chamber doors
- Gas detection alarms
- Cool-down protocols before venting

## Battery Research

### Lithium Metal Handling
**Hazards**:
- Highly reactive with water/air
- Fire risk (Class D fire)

**Required PPE**:
- Face shield
- Flame-resistant lab coat
- Dry gloves (no nitrile - can react)

**Engineering Controls**:
- Argon glovebox (O₂ < 0.5 ppm, H₂O < 0.5 ppm)
- Class D fire extinguisher nearby
- No water-based fire suppression

**Procedures**:
- Small quantities only
- Mineral oil storage for scraps
- Sand bucket for emergency

### Electrolyte Handling
**Hazards**:
- LiPF₆: Releases HF on hydrolysis
- Organic carbonates: Flammable
- Solid electrolytes: May contain sulfur (H₂S generation)

**Required Controls**:
- Fume hood or glovebox
- Calcium gluconate gel on hand (HF exposure)
- Moisture-free environment

### Cell Testing
**Hazards**:
- Thermal runaway
- Electrolyte venting
- Short circuit fires

**Required Controls**:
- Testing in ventilated enclosure
- Temperature monitoring with auto-shutoff
- Fire suppression blanket
- No unattended charging

## Hydrogen / Fuel Cell Research

### Hydrogen Gas Handling
**Hazards**:
- Flammable/explosive (4-75% in air)
- Colorless, odorless (no warning)
- Asphyxiation in enclosed spaces

**Required Controls**:
- H₂ detectors with audible alarm
- No ignition sources (sparks, flames)
- Ventilation (prevent accumulation)
- Explosion-proof electrical equipment

**Engineering Controls**:
- Outdoor cylinder storage
- Flow restrictors
- Emergency shutoff valves
- Grounding and bonding

### High-Temperature Operations (SOEC/SOFC)
**Hazards**:
- Temperatures 600-1000°C
- Hot surfaces
- Thermal shock

**Required PPE**:
- Heat-resistant gloves (rated to temperature)
- Face shield
- Heat-resistant apron

**Procedures**:
- Slow heating/cooling rates (1-5°C/min)
- Never touch without temperature check
- Allow complete cool-down before handling

### Electrolyzer Testing
**Hazards**:
- Electrical shock (high current)
- Pressure buildup
- Gas crossover

**Required Controls**:
- Electrical isolation procedures
- Pressure relief valves
- Gas separation verification
- Leak testing before operation

## Carbon Capture Research

### Amine Handling
**Hazards**:
- MEA, DEA: Corrosive, respiratory irritant
- Skin sensitizer
- Eye damage

**Required PPE**:
- Chemical splash goggles
- Nitrile gloves
- Lab coat

**Engineering Controls**:
- Fume hood for all handling
- Eye wash within 10 seconds
- Emergency shower

### Sorbent Regeneration
**Hazards**:
- High temperature (100-200°C for TSA)
- Vacuum (VSA)
- Pressure cycling

**Required Controls**:
- Temperature monitoring
- Pressure relief
- Proper vessel rating

### CO₂ Handling
**Hazards**:
- Asphyxiation (displaces O₂)
- Dry ice burns
- Pressure vessel risks

**Required Controls**:
- CO₂ monitors in enclosed spaces
- Cryogenic gloves for dry ice
- Rated pressure vessels

## Computational Work

### Minimal Physical Hazards, but:
- Ergonomic setup (repetitive strain)
- Eye strain (blue light exposure)
- Data security (backup protocols)

### High-Performance Computing
- Server room safety (electrical, cooling)
- UPS systems (battery hazards)
- Noise protection if needed

## Material-Specific Quick Reference

| Material | Primary Hazard | Key Control |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| Lead compounds | Toxic | Double gloves, fume hood |
| Lithium metal | Fire | Glovebox, Class D extinguisher |
| LiPF₆ | HF release | Dry handling, calcium gluconate |
| Hydrogen | Explosion | Detection, ventilation, grounding |
| Amines | Corrosive | Eye wash, emergency shower |
| Solvents (DMF, NMP) | Absorption | Fume hood, nitrile gloves |
| Nanoparticles | Inhalation | HEPA hood, N95 minimum |
| Strong acids | Burns | Face shield, apron, neutralizer |
| Strong bases | Burns | Face shield, apron |

## Incident Reporting Requirements

**Always Report**:
- Any injury requiring first aid
- Near misses
- Equipment failures
- Chemical spills
- Unexpected reactions

**Documentation Required**:
- Date, time, location
- Materials/equipment involved
- What happened
- Response taken
- Witnesses

**Report To**:
- Lab supervisor (immediate)
- Safety office (within 24 hours for injuries)
- EHS (for significant incidents)
